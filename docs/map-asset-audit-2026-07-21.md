# Overhead-map asset audit

Research cutoff: **2026-07-21**. No candidate listed here is wired into the live annotator merely because it exists.

## Acceptance rules

An annotation base must:

1. show the complete playable geometry needed by its prompt phase;
2. be a true top-down/overhead representation, not loading art or a perspective screenshot;
3. have a reproducible source URL, dimensions, retrieval date, immutable local filename, and SHA-256;
4. have a map-version statement and a visual regression review against current geometry;
5. have usable attribution/licensing metadata for public deployment.

## Staging verification

The 18 candidate responses represented in the source manifest were downloaded on 2026-07-21 to the durable `competitive-overhead-candidates` operator staging artifact outside this repository. The staged files total **7,765,357 bytes**. Their SHA-256 checksums were revalidated against `SHA256SUMS`, and their formats and dimensions were independently verified with `ffprobe` and recorded in the verified manifest. Antarctic Peninsula was verified as WebP and renamed to `antarctic-peninsula.webp` accordingly; the other 17 responses were verified as JPEG files.

Download, checksum, and format verification do **not** make any candidate production-approved. Geometry/map-version review and licensing clearance remain pending for every staged candidate. These files have not been copied into `map-annotator` and are not wired to `app.js`.

## Rejected material

- Loading-screen art and ordinary perspective screenshots from map galleries are not coordinate bases.
- A random GitHub repository was not executed. Repository trees and image files were inspected as read-only research data only; no third-party scripts, builds, or package commands were run.
- The unverified Midtown render is not a production asset until its creator/source and public-use terms are established.

## Current blocker

Candidate retrieval is complete for the 18 staged responses, but production approval remains blocked on geometry/map-version review and licensing clearance. Ten current maps still lack a usable public overhead, and many legacy top-downs predate current geometry. The prompt catalog can be authored independently, but production annotation must remain gated per map until an immutable, version-checked and license-cleared base is available.
