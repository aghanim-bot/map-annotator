# Overhead-map asset audit

Research cutoff: **2026-07-21**. No candidate listed here is wired into the live annotator merely because it exists.

## Acceptance rules

An annotation base must:

1. show the complete playable geometry needed by its prompt phase;
2. be a true top-down/overhead representation, not loading art or a perspective screenshot;
3. have a reproducible source URL, dimensions, retrieval date, immutable local filename, and SHA-256;
4. have a map-version statement and a visual regression review against current geometry;
5. have usable attribution/licensing metadata for public deployment.

## Staging verification

The 18 candidate responses represented in the source manifest were downloaded on 2026-07-21 to the durable `competitive-overhead-candidates` operator staging artifact outside this repository. The staged files total **7,765,357 bytes**. Their SHA-256 checksums were revalidated against `SHA256SUMS`, and their formats and dimensions were independently verified with `ffprobe` and recorded in the verified manifest. Antarctic Peninsula was verified as WebP and renamed to `antarctic-peninsula.webp` accordingly; the other 17 responses were verified as JPEG files.

Download, checksum, and format verification do **not** make any candidate production-approved. Geometry/map-version review and licensing clearance remain pending for every staged candidate. These files have not been copied into `map-annotator` and are not wired to `app.js`.

The recorded direct local pixel inspection in this audit is limited to Antarctic Peninsula. That image is an oblique exterior sky view rather than an orthographic floor plan: roofs, rocks, and occlusion hide traversable geometry, so it is **not coordinate-trustworthy** and cannot cover the three Control stages as an annotation base. The other 17 staged files have verified identities, formats, and dimensions, but this document does not claim that their pixels were individually inspected; their geometry verdicts remain pending.

## Licensing findings

The separately downloaded [Liquipedia Commons file page for Busan Top Down View](https://liquipedia.net/commons/File:Busan_Top_Down_View.jpg) identifies the file description as “Top down view of Overwatch maps,” names Statbanana as the author, gives `https://statbanana.com/images` as the source, and states: “This file is copyrighted, but used with permission of the rights holder.” The saved page response has SHA-256 `bb3552f7faaf599bbcf6f7573db6317735c5b4ab3024e305683c6fe7331b5a0a`.

This Busan page establishes a family-level rights risk for the other 14 legacy files; it does not verify their individual provenance or license terms. Each sibling requires its own file-page/source confirmation. Even for Busan, permission granted to Liquipedia does not establish permission for this project. None of the 15 files is publication-approved by this audit.

The separately downloaded Liquipedia Commons file pages for New Junk City and Suravasa state that the files are owned by Blizzard and used under linked Blizzard guidelines. Their saved page responses have SHA-256 `c67c0dc7be0c98af730705c076fe123844f4445b08ce84e898c88969aeae4042` and `ee76a616c51d50e5006327317ef67b3c2816d93532321b277d2645cb74957866`, respectively. The pages' old `http://blizzard.com/company/about/legal-faq.html` link currently redirects to [Blizzard Legal](https://www.blizzard.com/en-us/legal); the saved response for that destination has SHA-256 `9dcc247c38fec85844b01fb1817d08cf46e8c3a4e3732f96db3ae00dee5fca52`. That destination does not itself establish an explicit reusable image license, so both files remain blocked from publication pending confirmation under a current policy or direct permission.

All local or operator staging described in this audit is research-only. Possession of a downloaded response, a verified checksum, or a locally staged image is not authorization to publish it.

## Rejected material

- Loading-screen art and ordinary perspective screenshots from map galleries are not coordinate bases.
- A random GitHub repository was not executed. Repository trees and image files were inspected as read-only research data only; no third-party scripts, builds, or package commands were run.
- The unverified Midtown render is not a production asset until its creator/source and public-use terms are established.
